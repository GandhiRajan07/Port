Who am I?

I’m someone who’s always been drawn to structure—whether it was gears in a machine or rows in a spreadsheet. I studied mechanical engineering, and along the way, I realized that what fascinated me wasn’t just how systems worked—but how they could be optimized, predicted, and improved. That curiosity led me into data. Today, I’m someone who finds patterns in clutter, builds clarity from chaos, and enjoys turning questions into insights. I may be early in my data journey, but I bring with me an engineer’s mindset and a learner's drive to always keep learning.

about experience

Experience

My most recent role was in US healthcare billing—where I processed insurance claims, where every number has a weight, navigated structured data, and followed compliance rules to the letter. That experience gave me an unexpected appreciation for clean data—and a curiosity about the stories behind the numbers. I pursued a data science course, where I learned to write SQL queries, build visual dashboards in Tableau and Power BI, write Python scripts, and explore the basics of machine learning. Through hands-on projects, I moved from theory to practice—cleaning messy datasets, uncovering trends, and turning numbers into something meaningful. I'm still learning, still building—and ready to bring this growing toolkit into real-world problems.
